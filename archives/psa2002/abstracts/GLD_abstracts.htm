Chair    Elliott Sober (University of Wisconsin) 

 

"Exaptation and Function"    Elisabeth Lloyd (Indiana University) 

 

"Replaying Life's Tape"    John Beatty (University of Minnesota) 

In this paper I will consider critically, and I hope constructively, Stephen Gould's views on "historical contingency," together with ongoing efforts to "test" his views. Evaluating his position is less than straightforward for a couple of reasons. First, Gould articulated his position on contingency in two very different ways, without acknowledging that he was doing so. Moreover, in the process of evaluating his position(s), evolutionary biologists have not made it clear which of the two versions of contingency they are addressing.
